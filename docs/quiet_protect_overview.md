# Quiet Protect
## Shield the people you love from email fraud.

*Concept overview — March 2026 — for early feedback only, not for distribution*

---

## The problem

Email fraud targeting older and vulnerable adults is increasing rapidly in the UK. £1.2 billion was lost to fraud in 2023, and email remains the most common initial point of contact.

The tactics used are increasingly convincing. Phishing messages impersonate banks, HMRC, and delivery companies. Financial fraud emails create false urgency around payments or legal threats. Malicious links can compromise an entire device with a single click.

Older adults are disproportionately targeted. The people most at risk are often the least familiar with the tactics being used against them — and the consequences can be devastating.

Most email tools offer no protection against these threats. Basic junk filters catch obvious bulk mail. They don't understand fraud.

---

## What Quiet Protect is

Quiet Protect is a protection layer for people who are vulnerable to email-based fraud, managed by a trusted family member or carer.

It works for two people at once:

**For the person being protected** — their inbox looks cleaner and simpler. Suspicious emails are held back quietly before they can cause harm. Their experience of email improves without any changes required from them.

**For the family member or carer** — a simple dashboard shows how many suspicious emails have been flagged, what types of fraud were detected, and when Quiet last checked. Alerts arrive if something serious is identified.

---

## A note on privacy

Quiet Protect is designed with privacy at its core.

**The family member never sees the content of their relative's emails.**

The dashboard shows only threat summaries — how many suspicious messages were flagged, and what category of fraud was attempted. Personal messages, conversations, and correspondence remain completely private.

Protection does not require access. A family member can know their relative is being looked after without reading their inbox.

---

## How it works

### For the person being protected

**Connect** — Quiet Protect connects to their existing email account. Nothing changes about how they currently use email.

**Filter** — The Quiet AI assistant identifies emails showing signs of fraud and holds them aside. Known scam patterns, suspicious senders, and high-risk email types are caught automatically.

**Control** — Flagged emails are not deleted. Your relative can still choose to review them. They are protected, not managed.

### For the family member or carer

**Dashboard** — A simple view showing recent activity. How many suspicious emails were caught this week. What categories of threat were detected.

**Alerts** — If an unusually high volume of fraud attempts arrives, or something looks especially serious, you receive a notification. You don't need to check in daily.

**Reports** — Confirmed scam attempts can be reported to Action Fraud with a single tap, helping to protect others.

---

## The three pillars

**Block** — Known scam patterns and suspicious senders are automatically held back before they reach the inbox. No action needed from your relative.

**Review** — Borderline emails — those that look unusual but can't be confirmed as harmful — are flagged rather than silently delivered or deleted. A summary is available for the family member.

**Report** — Confirmed fraud attempts are logged and can be reported with a single tap. This contributes to national fraud intelligence and helps protect others facing the same threats.

---

## Who Quiet Protect is for

**Older and vulnerable adults** — People who use email regularly but may find it harder to identify modern fraud tactics. Quiet Protect helps without requiring any change to how they currently use email.

**Concerned family members and carers** — Adult children, relatives, or professional carers who want to provide a practical extra layer of protection — while fully respecting the person's independence and privacy.

The decision to use Quiet Protect should involve the person being protected. It works best when it's set up together as a shared, positive choice — not something imposed without discussion.

## Market context

Fraud protection for older adults is increasingly recognised as a genuine need, not a niche product. The combination of ageing demographics, rising digital dependency, and increasingly sophisticated fraud tactics creates a large and growing opportunity.

Quiet Protect addresses this not as a technical product but as a human one. The purchase decision is driven by love and concern. That is a very different — and very motivated — buyer.
