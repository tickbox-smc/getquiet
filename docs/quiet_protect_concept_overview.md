# Quiet Protect
## Peace of mind for your family.

---

> Email scams targeting elderly people cost UK victims hundreds of millions of pounds every year. The problem is not that people are careless. It is that the threat arrives looking exactly like something legitimate — and it arrives in the inbox before anyone can stop it.

Quiet Protect stops it before it arrives.

---

## The problem

Email fraud targeting older adults is one of the fastest-growing forms of financial crime in the UK. The most common attacks impersonate trusted organisations:

- **HMRC** — fake tax refunds, unpaid tax threats
- **Royal Mail / Parcelforce** — failed delivery, customs fee requests
- **Banks** — account suspended, unusual activity, verify details
- **BT / Sky / Virgin** — account cancellation, payment failed
- **TV Licensing** — licence expired, enforcement action
- **NHS** — appointment links, prescription requests

The victim sees a convincing email, acts on it, and the damage is done. By the time anyone finds out, money has been transferred or personal details compromised.

The standard advice — "be careful what you click" — does not work. These emails are designed by professionals to be indistinguishable from the real thing. Vigilance is not a realistic defence.

## What Quiet Protect does

Quiet Protect sits between the internet and your relative's inbox. Every email is evaluated before it is delivered. Scams are blocked silently. Legitimate emails are delivered normally.

**Your relative never sees the threat. They never know it arrived.**

You — the family manager — receive a weekly summary and an immediate alert if anything serious is blocked.

---

## Three things Quiet Protect does

**Block** — High-confidence scams are stopped before delivery. Your relative's inbox never contains them.

**Review** — Suspicious but uncertain emails are held for your decision. You deliver or block with one tap.

**Report** — A weekly digest tells you everything that happened. Delivered safely, suppressed quietly, blocked completely.

## How it works

Quiet Protect uses an AI scoring engine to evaluate every incoming email on two dimensions simultaneously.

### Sender trust

Every sender is evaluated against a trust model:

- Is this sender on the trusted list you have configured?
- Does the sending domain match the claimed organisation?
- Has this sender been flagged as suspicious before?

### Threat detection

Every email is scored for threat signals:

- Urgency and pressure language ("Act now", "Your account will be closed")
- Impersonation of known UK targets (HMRC, Royal Mail, banks, TV Licensing)
- Suspicious or spoofed sending domains (hmrc-refunds.co.uk, royalmai1-fees.com)
- Requests for personal information, passwords, or bank details
- Links to phishing pages
- Unexpected prize, lottery, or inheritance notifications
- Requests to call premium rate numbers

### Routing

| Threat score | Action |
|---|---|
| 0 — Safe | Delivered to your relative's inbox normally |
| 1–2 — Suspicious | Suppressed — added to your review queue |
| 3–5 — High confidence scam | Blocked — you receive an immediate alert |

## What you see as family manager

### The weekly digest

Every Sunday morning, you receive a summary:

> *"Geoffrey's inbox was safe this week. 12 emails were delivered from trusted contacts and safe senders. 4 potential scams were blocked before he saw them. 83 marketing and junk emails were quietly suppressed."*

The digest includes a plain-English description of every scam that was blocked — what it claimed to be, what signals were detected, and confirmation it never reached the inbox.

### Immediate scam alerts

When a high-confidence scam is detected (threat score 4 or 5), you receive an alert within minutes. It includes:

- The sender name and email address
- The subject line
- A plain-English explanation of why it was blocked
- The specific threat signals that triggered the block

Your relative is never told. They are never alarmed. The threat is simply gone.

### The review queue

Emails with a threat score of 1 or 2 — suspicious but not certain — are held for your review. You see the sender, subject, and the reason it was flagged. One tap to deliver, one tap to block.

For example: a genuine BT direct debit reminder may be flagged because payment urgency language is a common scam signal. You can review it, confirm it looks legitimate, and deliver it — or add BT to the trusted sender list so future emails come through automatically.

---

## The trusted sender list

You maintain a whitelist of senders who are always delivered, regardless of content. Set it up once and update it when needed.

Typical trusted senders:

- GP surgery and hospital
- Bank (verified sending address)
- Family members and close friends
- Utility providers (once verified)
- Trusted organisations — Age UK, RNIB, local council

Emails from trusted senders bypass all threat scoring. They arrive directly in the inbox, always.

## The same engine, a different purpose

Quiet Protect is not a separate product. It is Quiet — the autonomous email agent — running in protection mode.

The underlying technology is identical. The AI scoring engine, the Microsoft Graph API connection, the suppression logic — all the same. The difference is the scoring prompt and the interface.

In standard Quiet, the engine scores for importance and actionability — what needs your attention, what can be handled, what can be ignored.

In Quiet Protect, the engine scores for trust and threat — what is safe to deliver, what needs review, what must be blocked.

One platform. Two front doors.

| | Quiet | Quiet Protect |
|---|---|---|
| For | Anyone who wants to stop thinking about email | Families protecting elderly or vulnerable relatives |
| Core value | Save time | Prevent harm |
| Primary user | Themselves | Their relative |
| Who manages it | The user | The family member |
| Notifications | None | Weekly digest + immediate alerts |

## Why this matters

Roughly 11 million people over 65 live in the UK. A significant and growing proportion are active email users. The scams targeting them are increasingly sophisticated — designed by organised criminal groups, localised to the UK, and updated regularly to avoid detection.

No existing product addresses this from the family's perspective. Spam filters help but are bypassable. Advice campaigns reach some people but not at the moment of threat. Quiet Protect intercepts the threat before the person ever sees it — which is the only intervention that actually works.

The people who will pay for this are not the elderly users themselves. They are the adult children and grandchildren who worry about this problem and currently have no tool to help. The purchase decision is driven by love and concern, not by productivity. That is a very different — and very motivated — buyer.
